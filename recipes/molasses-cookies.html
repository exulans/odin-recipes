Molasses cookies

Description

Crumbly cookies - not gooey, not crisp.

Ingredients

  • ½ tablespoon vinegar
  • ½ cup milk
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ½ cup (8 tablespoons) shortening (or lard, or soft unsalted butter)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup molasses
  • 4 cups all purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoon baking soda
  • ¼ teaspoon cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon ginger

Steps

  1. Grease or parchment-line cookie sheets.
  2. Combine the vinegar and milk (this is a subsitution for sour milk).
  3. Combine the baking soda, cinnamon, ginger, and 2½ cups of flour.
  4. Cream together the sugar and shortening until well-combined with a spatula.
  5. Beat in the molasses and egg until smooth.
  6. Alternately mix in the dry and milk mixtures, starting and ending with the dry mixture.
  8. Mix in the rest of the flour.
  9. Chill the batter in the refigerator for at least an hour
  10. Form cookies by putting a pint-sized blob of batter onto a flour-dusted surface. Dust the top of the dough with flour as you squish it down, then roll it out with a rolling pin.
  11. Cut out shapes, then place them on the cookie sheet.
  12. Bake cookies for 8–10 minutes at 375°F.
